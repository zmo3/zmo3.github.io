Bone X-ray Abnormal Study Detection

EECS 349 Machine Learning Fall 2018
Zhenguo Mo: zhenguomo2021@u.northwestern.edu

The task is to determine from a set of x-ray images if there is an abnormal study, which we have narrowed down to determining the presence of an abnormality from a shoulder x-ray. If done well, this can potentially assist in radiology research and help medical professionals work faster. This is also a competition hosted by Stanford ML Group earlier this year (2018), and there have already been models submitted that can perform better than radiologists. It is also exciting to be exploring computer vision concepts in this project.

We decided to build our model using a Convolutional Neural Network. It seemed to be the most effective classifier that handles image inputs. We experimented with the design of the Convolutional Neural Net and logged the training and validation accuracies and losses as training progressed. Most designs tended to have higher than 70% training accuracy, but the best logged validation accuracy was 68%. The validation accuracies fluctuated a lot from the lack of data after taking only a subset of the provided dataset, and it seems that the model did not generalize well to new examples. This is due to limitations in the processing time and equipment used. We document our analysis in more detail in the report.
